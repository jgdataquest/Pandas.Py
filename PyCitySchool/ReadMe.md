
PyCity Schools Analysis 

OBSERVED TREND for overall school students' Math and Reading performance 
1. The reading scores is higher than Math. The scores (reading vs math) - 82 vs 72, The passing rates (reading vs math) - 83% vs 72%.
2. The top schools passing rate is over 90% but bottom schools are around 70%. The gap is significantly showed.
3. The scores with different grades show consistent performance in all the school.

OBSERVED TREND for school comparison
1. The school size affect student scores dramatically. The schools with under 2000 students show the math and reading score significant better comparing with the school size between 2000 – 5000.
2. The school budget didn't show the influence on students test scores neither on math nor reading. The records even show the more budget the worse scores. However, the budget may not include private donation or the family income etc that may use to help students on academic performance.
3. The school types show the student test scores differently.  Top 5 schools are all charter schools and bottom 5 are all district schools.

District Summary


```python
school="raw_data/schools_complete.csv"
school_pd = pd.read_csv(school)
school_pd.rename(columns = {'name':'school'}, inplace = True)
students="raw_data/students_complete.csv"
students_pd = pd.read_csv(students)
df = pd.merge(school_pd, students_pd, on=("school"))
total_school=school_pd.count().max()
total_students=students_pd['name'].count()
total_budget=school_pd['budget'].sum()
#Average Math Score
avg_math_score = df['math_score'].sum()/total_students
avg_math_score
#Average Reading Score
avg_reading_score = df['reading_score'].sum()/total_students
avg_reading_score
#% Passing Math
passing_math_percentage =df[df.math_score > 70].count().math_score * 100/total_students
#df[cols] > 0).sum(1)                       
passing_math_percentage
#% Passing Reading
passing_reading_percentage =df[df.reading_score > 70].count().reading_score * 100/total_students
passing_reading_percentage
#Overall Passing Rate (Average of the above two)
#overall_passing_rate = (df[df.reading_score > 70].count().reading_score +\
#                        df[df.math_score > 70].count().math_score) * 100/(2*total_students)
overall_passing_rate = (passing_reading_percentage + passing_math_percentage)/2
overall_passing_rate
# Create a new table consolodating above calculations
dist_summary = pd.DataFrame({"Total Schools": [total_school],
                                   "Total Students": [total_students],
                                   "Total Budget": [total_budget],
                                   "Average Math Score": [avg_math_score],
                                   "Average Reading Score": [avg_reading_score],
                                   "% Passing Math":[passing_math_percentage],
                                   "% Passing Reading":[passing_reading_percentage],
                                   "Overall Passing Rate": [overall_passing_rate]
                            })
dist_summary = dist_summary [["Total Schools","Total Students","Total Budget","Average Math Score",\
                             "Average Reading Score","% Passing Math","% Passing Reading",\
                             "Overall Passing Rate"]]
dist_summary["Total Budget"] = dist_summary["Total Budget"].map("${0:,.0f}".format)
dist_summary
```

```python
## School Summary
school_pd['Per Student Budget']=school_pd['budget'] / school_pd['size']

indexed_school_pd = school_pd.set_index(['school'])
indexed_school_pd['Average Math Score']=students_pd.groupby('school')['math_score'].mean()
indexed_school_pd['Average Reading Score']=students_pd.groupby('school')['reading_score'].mean()
#% Passing Math
math_passing_count=students_pd[students_pd.math_score > 70].groupby('school')['math_score'].count()

#Overall Passing Rate (Average of the above two)
indexed_school_pd['% Overall Passing Rate'] = \
           (indexed_school_pd['% Passing Math'] + indexed_school_pd['% Passing reading'])/2
del indexed_school_pd['School ID']
indexed_school_pd.rename(columns = {'type':'School Type', 'size':'Total Students','budget':'Total School Budget'}, inplace = True)
```
